Kami (紙, かみ) means paper in Japanese: the surface where a finished idea lands. AI can produce documents better than most humans do manually.

The missing piece is not capability but constraint: without a design system, every session drifts into generic gray and inconsistent layouts.

Kami fills that gap: one constraint language, nine templates, simple enough for agents to run reliably, strict enough that every output is something you actually want to ship.

Part of a trilogy: Kaku writes code, Waza drills habits, Kami delivers documents.

Warm parchment canvas #f5f4ed, ink blue #1B365D as the sole accent, serif carries hierarchy, no hard shadows or flashy palettes.

Documents should read as composed pages, not dashboards. Not a UI framework; a constraint system for printed matter.
